ILCL: Inverse Logic-Constraint Learning from Temporally Constrained Demonstrations
Minwoo Cho, Jaehwi Jang, Daehyung Park
RIRO Lab, KAIST
Paper Code BibTeX
Abstract
Effectively integrating constraints into reinforcement learning (RL) is crucial for safe and reliable deployment in real-world applications. However, manually designing these constraints is challenging, particularly for complex, temporally-extended behaviors. In this work, we propose Inverse Logic-Constraint Learning (ILCL), a framework that learns interpretable temporal logic constraints from demonstrations and incorporates them into RL to improve task performance while ensuring constraint satisfaction. Our approach consists of two key components: (1) a tree-based genetic algorithm that efficiently searches the space of temporal logic formulas to identify constraints that are consistently observed across demonstrations, and (2) a constraint-regularized RL algorithm with logic cost redistribution that enables training policies with zero constraint violations. Experiments in both simulated and real-world robotic tasks demonstrate that ILCL successfully learns meaningful constraints from demonstrations, leading to improved generalization performance and zero-violation policies in complex domains.
Method

Our method, Inverse Logic-Constraint Learning (ILCL), learns interpretable temporal logic constraints from demonstrations and incorporates them into reinforcement learning. The approach consists of two main components:

  1. Inverse Logic-Constraint Learning: Using a tree-based genetic algorithm to search the space of temporal logic formulas and identify constraints that are consistently satisfied in demonstrations.
  2. Constraint-Regularized RL with Logic Cost Redistribution: A novel constraint-enforcing RL algorithm that leverages the learned constraints to guide policy optimization towards zero-violation solutions.

The temporal logic formulas provide interpretable constraints that can capture complex temporal dependencies in the demonstrated behaviors. Our approach can express a wide range of safety conditions and task specifications through these logical constraints.

Results
Constraint Learning

Our method successfully learns meaningful temporal logic constraints from demonstrations. These constraints accurately capture the underlying safety conditions and task specifications demonstrated by experts.

Policy Performance

Policies trained with ILCL demonstrate superior generalization to new task variations while maintaining zero constraint violations, outperforming baseline approaches in terms of both task success and safety.

Real-World Robotics

We validate our approach on real robotic systems, demonstrating the practical applicability of ILCL for learning safe policies in physical environments with complex constraints.

Our experiments across various domains demonstrate that ILCL effectively learns constraints from demonstrations and successfully incorporates them into policy optimization to achieve zero-violation policies.
